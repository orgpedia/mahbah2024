Introduction: The land of Maharashtra is witness to many historical events. It saw many battles even before the 1857 war of independence. The revolutionary king Umaji Naik was the one who saved the British through a similar struggle and struggled to make the dream of independence a reality. This year marks 350 years since the coronation of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The Hindu Swaraj, founded by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, included a large number of Vimukt caste and nomadic community members in the intelligence department. The revolutionary king Umaji Naik, who fought against the British, belonged to the Ramoshi community, one of the Vimukt Jati B nomadic tribes. Therefore, it was under the consideration of the Government to confer the "Krantiveera Raja Umaji Naik Award" in his name on individuals and organizations working in the field of art, social enlightenment and literature for the social, educational and economic development of the nomadic community of the state.
Government Decision: - Ma. As per the decision taken in the Cabinet meeting dated 10/10/2024, the Government is approving the scheme of conferring the award in the name of "Krantiveer Raje Umaji Naik Award" on individuals and organizations working in the field of social, artistic, social enlightenment and literary works for the social, educational, more development of the Vimukt Jati and Nomadic Tribes community in the State.
The rules regarding the selection, criteria, qualifications, rules, etc. of the award are being prescribed as mentioned in Annexure A, B, C, etc.
